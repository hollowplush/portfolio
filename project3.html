A calm, handdrawn animation of a small goat god.

✧✧✧

I've always had much respect for animation of any and all kinds. One artist I look up to particularly much is James Baxter, an animator who has animated multiple beautiful shots for Disney and other studios. After watching some of his animation reels, I decided I felt inspired to try some detailed animation as well.

I've already done multiple simple animations and animatics in the past - check out my own animation reel. However, I never had the motivation nor tools to work on animations with heavy shading and all that. Coincidentally, Procreate Dreams had just released not too long ago, and I realized I might get pretty far using it.

Next came the coloring, likely my less favorite part. It takes long, it's tedious, and it's difficult to color fur markings and such.

I wish Procreate Dreams was a better animation program, because it's actually awful for doing the actual animations. I do the animation in Procreate, and do the shading, lighting, and other effects in Dreams.

Finally, I finished the coloring. Free at last. Now, the fun can start.

I exported the gif from Procreate and imported it in Dreams.

I tried to create a background in Dreams initially, when I realized I was struggling too hard over it and switched back to Procreate to do it.

It is simply a still image, so no problems there. I spent the evening making a background and referenced this image for it.

I put an image of the gif on top of it to make sure it all aligns well and doesn't become too messy or crowded.

A background blur works wonders for keeping the focus on the main subject.

Finally, I could ACTUALLY begin working on the shading and lighting. For each frame, I added some simple shadows, and I put some gradients over the gif. Adding the lighting was my favorite part. I love making art look shiny and light.

In the background, I decided to add some floating petals. This helps sell the idea there's wind, despite the background image not moving. I ended up needing a lot of layers, displayed on the timeline below.

Procreate Dreams's exporting is not good. I had to save the different frames individually and use the gif option in Procreate. Of course, this also wasn't perfect. It ended up looking pretty crunchy and pixelated.

After a lot of trial and error, I found that it looks less bad when exporting a bigger canvas. So, I created a canvas as big as I could and stopped tinkering with it. I also learned about GIFs as a whole. Initially, I didn't know they simplify colors to save on resources...

It was fun to make this, but also time-consuming. I learned a lot though, and I'm very happy with my first detailed animation like this. I have a better understanding of all the work that goes into animation, which has boosted my appreciation of art even further.
